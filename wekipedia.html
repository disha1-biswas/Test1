Overview

YouTube is an American online video sharing and social media platform headquartered in San Bruno, California. It was launched on February 14, 2005, by Steve Chen, Chad Hurley, and Jawed Karim. It is the second most visited website in the world, after Google Search.

History

YouTube was created by three former PayPal employees. In November 2006, it was bought by Google for $1.65 billion. Since then, it has expanded significantly, adding features like live streaming, shorts, and premium subscriptions.

Features

YouTube allows users to upload, view, rate, share, add to playlists, report, comment on videos, and subscribe to other users. It offers different video quality levels, monetization options for creators, and mobile apps for various platforms.

Impact

YouTube has transformed entertainment, education, and marketing. It gave rise to a new profession: the YouTuber. It’s also a powerful tool for activism, cultural exchange, and journalism.
